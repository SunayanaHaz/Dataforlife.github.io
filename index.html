Where Technology Fails

An Accessibility Analysis of Real-World Websites

Team: Data4Lyf

Datathon: DubsTech Datathon 2026

Problem

Many websites unintentionally block people with disabilities. These barriers are invisible to designers but very real for users who rely on screen readers, keyboard navigation, or have low vision.

Dataset

  • 3,524 accessibility violations
  • ~591 unique web pages
  • Domains: News, Government, Health, Education, Technology, E-commerce

Key Findings

  • News & Media sites have the highest number of accessibility violations
  • A small set of issues (contrast, landmarks, link text) cause most problems
  • Severity matters more than raw counts

Risk Score

We introduced a severity-weighted Risk Score to prioritize pages that cause the greatest harm to users.

  • 5 × Critical
  • 4 × Serious
  • 3 × Moderate
  • 2 × Minor

Recommendations

  • Fix color contrast using WCAG standards
  • Add semantic landmarks for navigation
  • Ensure meaningful link text and alt text
  • Validate HTML structure and heading hierarchy

Conclusion

Accessibility failures are not random. They follow patterns, affect real people, and can be fixed with data-driven prioritization.
