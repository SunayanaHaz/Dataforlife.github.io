Where Technology Fails

An Accessibility Analysis of Real-World Websites

Team: Data4Lyf   |   Datathon: DubsTech Datathon 2026

Problem

Many websites unintentionally block people with disabilities. These barriers are invisible to designers but very real for users who rely on screen readers, keyboard navigation, or have low vision.

Dataset

  • 3,524 accessibility violations
  • ~591 unique web pages
  • Domains: News & Media, Government, Health, Education, Technology, E-commerce

Key Findings

  • Uneven distribution: News & Media shows the highest volume of violations.
  • Repeat offenders: Contrast, landmarks, link text, duplicate IDs, and headings dominate.
  • Impact > volume: Severity-weighted Risk Score highlights the most harmful pages.

Risk Score

We introduced a severity-weighted Risk Score to prioritize pages that cause the greatest harm:

  • 5 × Critical
  • 4 × Serious
  • 3 × Moderate
  • 2 × Minor

Recommendations

  • Fix color contrast: Enforce WCAG contrast thresholds with automated checks in design tools and CI.
  • Add semantic landmarks: Standardize <nav>, <main>, <header>, <footer> across templates.
  • Meaningful link text: Replace generic links with descriptive labels; add alt text for image-links.
  • Validate structure: Enforce unique IDs and heading hierarchy via linting/testing.

Conclusion

Accessibility failures are not random. They follow patterns, affect real people, and can be fixed with data-driven prioritization.
